Student-Athlete

Swimming

I have been swimming competitively for 10 years, so it is a significant part of who I am. It has guided me through so many stressors in my life, helped me become more focused and motivated outside of the pool. I am always at my best when I am swimming. It has constantly challenged me, but it has pushed me to be much stronger than I ever thought I could be. I can truthfully say that my life would be completely different without the impact of my team, and the sport itself. Swimming has helped mold me into the person I am today.

Accolades

In swimming, I earned a letter in all 3 seasons (and counting) of high school. I received the High Point award for most points scored on my team in my sophomore and junior year. I was a WPIAL qualifier in the 50 Freestyle, 200 Medley Relay, 200 Free Relay, and 400 Free Relay my freshman year. Our 200 Freestyle Relay tied for 8th at theWPIAL Championships, so I got to take home a medal.

Sophomore year, I qualified in the 50 Freestyle, 100 Breaststroke, 200 Medley Relay, and 200 Free Relay. That year, we placed 7th in the 200 Freestyle Relay. I qualified for the same events in my junior season. This year, I am a senior captain. In Volleyball, I lettered my junior and senior year on the varsity team. I was a Captain my senior year, and made Third Team WPIAL All Section 5 AA.

Volleyball

I started playing volleyball when I joined my middle school team in 7th grade. The fast- thinking, high energy sport was a new pace that I had really enjoyed. I decided to keep playing, and move on to my high school team. It was a completely different game at that level, and our team improved very quickly beginning in high school. Throughout those 6 years, I had grown to love the sport. It taught me about the value of a team, and how your success depends on the success of the team. Volleyball remains a nostalgic memory of being with my friends, and working towards a goal together.
