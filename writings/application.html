College Application Essay

Lauren Angus
If you could give one piece of advice to incoming high school students what would it be?

It is fair to say that I was truely my toughest critic growing up and starting high school. My mom was always tough on me to always try my hardest on absolutely everything that I did. She implemented the mindset in me that I shouldn’t make excuses for myself and to take responsibility for my actions. I’ll be honest, a lot of my success is due to that mindset.

However, as I became more accountable for myself and more independent, it became really hard to hear how great I need to be from everyone. The school has a job to make kids accountable for themselves and push them, so that was another place keeping me in line. Here’s where I stood: I had the school, my parents, and mostly myself telling me how good I should be. At times, that was more pressure than I could handle. After my first year of high school, I got lazier than I ever had been because I had grown tired of it. I couldn’t worry or stress myself out like I did in the past. I was mentally exhausted, and that affected my attitude. Granted, part of that exhaustion came from learning how to juggle volleyball, swimming, choir, ensemble, musical, and being class secretary. That year, I still did very well in school, but I knew that I could do better.

If I could give an incoming high school student similar to me one piece of advice, I would tell them a simple “you’re doing okay, it’s going to be okay.” For motivated high school students, reassurance is the best thing you can give them. I’d be the person for them who’d be by their side to let them know that they’re doing just fine. I’d give them the confidence they didn’t know they would need to get through high school. Most of my struggles academically, socially, personally, and athletically in school have been due to self-doubt, uncertainty, and lack of confidence in myself. This is something that I see common with a lot of students that isn’t discussed enough. The underlying pressure that causes us to doubt ourselves is one of the main roadblocks in high school. Between figuring out what our new found high school personalities are, keeping up with our social lives amongst high school drama, deciding what’s next after high school when we have to abruptly mature into adults, and maintaining good grades, our minds become choatic. This is something I personally relate to a lot, being a person with a very active mind and a filled schedule.

It took me a while to figure it out, but now I am equipped with the balance I need to take on my ambitions. I couldn’t imagine not being a part of many activities and still striving to be my best. So to keep up with the life that I imagine for myself, I will continue to improve my time management and maintain a balanced lifestyle. I also will remind myself that I can do these things in moments of self-doubt.
