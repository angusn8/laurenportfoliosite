Effective Followership

In order to be a successful group, there must be a solid dynamic of leaders and followers. Typically, the group is judged by the effectiveness of their leaders. However, a leader would be nothing without their dependable collection of followers. Therefore, we must also take into account the effectiveness of the followers to determine the strength of the group. To be an effective follower is to not only be an active member of the union established by the leader, but to question the effectiveness of the leader when necessary. A leader who is immune to criticism is often corrupt, and in reality, much further from perfection than one who is challenged in a formal manner. The responsibility of maintaining the leader’s effectiveness is held by only the most effective followers.

When people blindly follow the leader and provide unconditional support, the leader then has free reign to take advantage of their people and act poorly. This is not to say that followers shouldn’t be supportive, but to highlight the idea that they can be supportive while still giving constructive criticism when their leader has made a mistake, or is not performing to the best of their abilities. This idea has been proven to be an essential way to maintain a strong union in even the largest groups of people. It is used to weed out dictators in various countries, and is even written in their historical documents to ensure that it is not lost. America's founding fathers themselves knew the importance of effective followership when they included several opportunities to question and evolve the government in the constitution. In the first amendment, they specifically protected the freedom to petition the government. They also allowed for the freedom of speech and press, and consequently, people now have the ability to speak out on the success or failure of our government. Much too often, these freedoms are abused by people who are ill-intended, or ineffective in the way they present their opinion. However, the freedoms must remain for the sake of the other followers who are trying to instill positive change, and for the nation to be enduring and strong. Effective followership is utilizing these opportunities in an intelligent manner for the common good of the group.

Effective followership can also mean to be an active supporter, instead of a silent one. A leader always needs more people to help get their ideas spread and accepted among the group. Active supporters can relate to the other followers and act as a connection between the leadership and followership. This encourages the followers to carry out the leaders policies, and be productive in their work. Being productive is how the silent followers can be effective. Active followers are great communicators, and are an essential element of the leader- follower dynamic. Because they step up when needed, they can also be considered leaders in a way. Without them, the leaders would not know how they are perceived within the group, and the silent followers would have difficulty communicating how they are being led. It would also be difficult for the other followers to do their job if the leaders' policies were ineffective, which is where the active followers come in to communicate. All followers are equally important, but those who speak up are a group to be recognized.They are star players, and the team would be much less effective without them.

Sometimes followers feel that their role isn’t important because they are not in charge. This is indefinitely wrong, as they have strength in numbers, as well as important roles to play. It is known that if the leadership is ineffective, the group suffers. The same is true for all types of followers. If they do not contribute to the well being of the group by doing their part, no matter how big or small, it will be weaker because of it. This makes effective followership just as important as effective leadership.
